# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Ethylene glycol (antifreeze) poisoning over two weeks
**Category:** Murder (Fellow Traveler - premeditated)
**Duration:** Two weeks of slow poisoning, final organ failure over 48 hours
**Witnesses:** Hospital staff for final moments; the poisoning itself was invisible

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Ethylene glycol poisoning. The sweet-tasting compound is metabolized into glycolic acid and oxalic acid, causing metabolic acidosis, central nervous system depression, and acute kidney failure through calcium oxalate crystal deposition.

### The Setup
Petra Mueller, a German travel photographer with a failing blog, has shared a dorm room with Maddie for three days when the poisoning begins. Watching Maddie's easy success - the brand deals, the followers, the effortless beauty - triggers something in Petra. She sources antifreeze from a local mechanic's shop. The bright green liquid, when mixed into smoothies or green tea, is undetectable.

### The Trigger
Maddie receives a DM from a luxury resort offering her a $10,000 partnership. She squeals about it in the dorm. Petra congratulates her, goes to the bathroom, and begins planning the first dose.

### The Process

1. Day 1: First dose in a shared smoothie. Maddie feels vaguely unwell, attributes it to Bali belly
2. Days 2-5: Small daily doses in tea, smoothies, sometimes direct in her water bottle. Maddie feels increasingly sick but assumes it's a virus
3. Days 6-10: Larger doses. Maddie's vision blurs, she vomits frequently, has difficulty walking. Petra "nurses" her
4. Days 11-13: Maddie can barely leave bed. Petra suggests she's just dehydrated, keeps bringing drinks
5. Day 14: Maddie collapses in the hostel kitchen, is rushed to hospital
6. Day 15: Doctors identify antifreeze poisoning, begin treatment, but kidney damage is too severe
7. Day 16: Multiple organ failure, death

## Research Notes
- Ethylene glycol has a sweet taste, easily masked in fruity drinks
- Initial symptoms mirror food poisoning or flu
- Without treatment, lethal dose is approximately 100ml
- Chronic small doses cause cumulative damage before acute crisis
- Detection requires specific toxicology testing not routinely done
- Famous poisoning cases include Stacey Castor (NY) and Kristin Rossum (CA)

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Maddie has been sick for twelve days. Her followers are worried - she's posted nothing but wellness content and apologies. Petra has been by her side constantly, bringing teas and smoothies, posting updates to Maddie's story on her behalf. "You need to keep your engagement up," Petra says, smiling.

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
Maddie collapses in the kitchen trying to make toast. Her legs simply stop working. She falls hard, unable to break her fall. Other guests call an ambulance. Petra rides with her, holding her hand.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)

**Hours 1-12:**
Hospital in Denpasar. Doctors assume food poisoning, begin standard treatment. Maddie is confused, scared. Petra visits, brings her phone so she can post. "Your followers need to know you're okay."

**Hours 12-24:**
Maddie worsens. Kidneys failing. Doctors order expanded toxicology after finding unusual crystals in her urine. Petra's visits become shorter. She mentions she might "check out soon, give you space."

**Hours 24-36:**
Results come back: ethylene glycol. Doctors begin treatment but warn damage may be irreversible. Police are notified. When they ask about her roommate, Maddie's blood runs cold. Suddenly, she remembers: the sweet smoothies, Petra's insistence she finish every drink, the way Petra smiled when she vomited.

**Final Hours:**
Maddie's parents are flying in but won't make it. She's conscious, barely. Petra is gone - checked out of the hostel, destination unknown. Maddie's phone buzzes: a DM from @PetraShots that reads "Your account will be dead by tomorrow. Mine won't be." She doesn't have the strength to respond.

**Final Moment:**
Multiple organ failure. Her mother is on the phone with the hospital when Maddie codes. The last thing she sees is the fluorescent lights of the ICU. The last thing she thinks is: I just wanted people to like me.

### Immediate Aftermath
Petra is arrested at Ngurah Rai airport attempting to fly to Thailand. Maddie's followers spike by 200,000 in the days after her death - the tragic story going viral. Her account becomes a memorial. The hostel is investigated but found not liable. Petra's own Instagram gains 50,000 followers in the weeks before her trial - exactly what she wanted.

## Sensory Details

### Sight
The harsh hospital lights. The fluid draining into bags by her bed - wrong colors. Her phone screen, blurry. The faces of doctors trying to hide their concern. The ceiling tiles she stares at for hours.

### Sound
The beeping of monitors - too fast, then too slow. Nurses speaking Indonesian she doesn't understand. Her mother's voice on the phone, cracking.

### Touch/Pain
The constant nausea, like motion sickness that never stops. The IV needles. The strange numbness spreading through her body. The hospital sheets, rough against skin.

### Smell
Hospital antiseptic. Her own sickness. The phantom memory of Petra's smoothies - suddenly sinister.

### Taste
Metallic, wrong. The sweet aftertaste of those drinks, now haunting her. The tube in her throat.

## The Horror Element
The horror is intimacy betrayed - Maddie trusted Petra completely, ate what she made, drank what she offered, let her into her phone, her life. The horror is the invisible nature of the attack: you can be murdered while thinking you're being loved.

## The Comedy Element
Dark irony: Maddie's death finally makes her go viral - she's more famous dead than alive. Her account gains more followers in death than her last three months of work. Petra wanted fame and got it - as a murderer.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
- Very slow build - the poisoning unfolds over weeks in the narrative
- Intersperse Petra's POV chapters showing her planning and satisfaction
- The hospital sequence should be disorienting, reflecting Maddie's mental state
- Death itself should feel like slipping away, not dramatic

### POV During Death
- Mostly Maddie's POV, increasingly unreliable as toxins affect her brain
- Brief Petra POV for the ironic distance
- Hospital staff POV for clinical aftermath

### Graphic Content Level
- 5/10: Focus on suffering and betrayal rather than graphic details
- The symptoms should be medically accurate but not gratuitous
- Emotional devastation over physical horror
